MY STORY

I started working in Photoshop and Illustrator to create tour posters and album covers for my band that you’ve definitely never heard of. My first HTML/CSS class had me hooked, so I started making static websites for small businesses. I took any opportunity to design a logo, a t-shirt, or other promo material for anyone that needed it.

It wasn’t until reading The Design of Everyday Things by Don Norman did I fully understand that design is not just about perfect alignment, an appealing palette, or exuding cool. Design is about making things better - more useful and more pleasurable to use. Design doesn’t happen in a vacuum. Research should inform design decisions, and user testing should hash out potential.

From a parking app to how we pay our bills, smart design helps us live better. Within this scope, I intend to utilize the vast capacity of user experience design to build useful and pleasurable web and mobile applications for the world. I aim to focus on advocating for accessibility while recognizing that some users will interact with products differently than how we would expect.
