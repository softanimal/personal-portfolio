SpinDen

Track song plays and social media followers - all in one place.
Built for independent musicians.

CASE STUDY

Streaming technology has driven a rapid evolution of the music industry including the ability to track and analyze who is listening, and how they are listening. Big data metrics have popped up on the market targeting music industry executives and up-and-coming artists with deep dive analytics. SpinDen aims to diverge from the pack of applications competing for industry standardization by creating an accessible and useful tool for musicians at every level of success.

VIEW PROTOTYPE

Problem

The average musician is overwhelmed with data and insights from the top metrics applications on the market. Yet, without access to an application that conglomerates metrics, musicians are required to log in to dozens of online platforms in order to track their key stats - play counts and followers.

Solution

SpinDen was specifically designed to target the average musician who feels bogged down by big data metrics yet still needs uncomplicated access to their core metrics. Moreover, SpinDen is one of the only mobile apps on the market; giving users an essential connection to their music reach.

RESEARCH & RESULTS

I created a user research survey that focused on the following elements: Preferred streaming platforms, importance of metrics, tracking rates, and desktop vs. mobile.

  • 75% of recording musicians have their music available for online streaming
  • Soundcloud, Youtube and Spotify are the 3 most popular streaming platforms
  • 56% of respondents check their streaming metrics once a week
  • 0% check their metrics via mobile only

Takeaway: The vast majority of musicians have their music available for online streaming, but not a large catalog of material. They have intermediate interest in their stats, and are mostly logging in via desktop.

BRANDING & LOGO

I designed two variations of a logo which were preference tested by a random sample of participants. The preferred choice was the logo within a circle with a line chart representing the “S” in SpinDen. But after further testing, the “S” was determined to be illegible...so the logo was paired down to a more uniform and stable design.

VIEW LOGO USAGE >

COLOR PALETTE

A muted dark tone was chosen to represent the foundation of the design. Two accent colors were chosen to showcase user action, play counts and follower counts.

TYPOGRAPHY

Fredoka One was chosen for the Logo font due to its rounded frame. Lato was chosen as the main font for its strength. Didact Gothic was chosen as a subfont for its refinement and clarity.

CONCLUSION

The SpinDen mobile app was an original idea. In comparison to previous projects, I spent more time documenting the creative process, and better honed in on the phases of user testing and design iteration.

THINGS I LEARNED

I learned there can be no limit on the amount of user testing, and how incredibly valuable any user can be to the design process. If I could further develop the SpinDen app, I would tackle more user stories; adding more functionality. I would also continue examining the onboarding process.
